What it does

  • Kills AI-slop phrases: leverage, seamlessly, cutting-edge, elevate, journey, synergy, delve into.
  • Injects human markers: contractions, informal abbreviations (tbh, imo, w/), lowercase sentence starts, fragments.
  • Drops structural tells: three-item lists, "not X, but Y" rhythms, rhetorical question endings.
  • 120 reference samples: Paul Graham essays, HN top-karma comments, pre-2022 Twitter.
  • 20 anti-slop examples: marked NEVER-write-like-this.

Why this exists

I spent two weeks building an AI-slop detector. Failed. F1=0.27 — below random. The model learned "short text = AI" because my slop corpus was short LinkedIn posts and my human corpus was long-form essays. Length confounded class. No feature fix saved it.

The lesson wasn't technical. I framed the problem wrong. "Detect AI" is commodity. The actually useful product was the inverse: rewrite drafts so they don't sound AI. A voice guide, a banned-phrase list, and a corpus of good human writing as the moat — not a classifier.

Two days of pivoting got me this skill. It works on my drafts. I'm shipping it before I polish it more, because that's the right order.
